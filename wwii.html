The Beginning of Tank Warfare

World War II contained the first real instances of what we today would call tank versus tank combat. Leading up to and including the years of the war, tanks evolved rapidly from slow, weakly armored, and sporting sub-50mm cannons to tanks capable of rapid deployment, armor to bounce enemy shells, and guns upwards of 76mm that could easily dispatch enemy armor. This rapid development carried on until around the 1990s, where it slowed down somewhat.

The most produced tanks from the main countries of World War II were:

  1. M4 Sherman (49,000)
  2. T-34 (35,000)
  3. Panzer IV (8,500)
